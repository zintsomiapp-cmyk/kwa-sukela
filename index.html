About the Academy

A Voice That
Carries History

"Storytelling is not entertainment alone — it is the archive of a people, the curriculum of a culture."

With a Master of Arts in Storytelling from Tennessee State University and a PhD in Folklore from the University of the Witwatersrand, Dr. Nomsa Mdlalose has spent over two decades at the intersection of tradition and teaching.

Known across Southern Africa as "Dr. Sasa," she is the founder of Zintsomi, the creator of Africa's first accredited storytelling course, and an Imbokodo Award-winning voice for African women's narratives.

The Storyteller Behind the Academy

Dr. Nomsa Mdlalose

NM
Dr. Nomsa Mdlalose
Dr. Sasa · Master Storyteller, Author & Folklorist

"The story does not begin with the storyteller. It begins the moment the listener leans in."

Dr. Nomsa Mdlalose — known widely as "Dr. Sasa" — is one of South Africa's most celebrated storytelling academics and practitioners. With over two decades at the intersection of tradition, education, and performance, she has carved a unique space in African cultural life.

Holding a Master of Arts in Storytelling from Tennessee State University and a PhD in Folklore from the University of the Witwatersrand, her academic grounding is matched only by her warmth and magnetism as a performer. She has told stories on stages from rural KwaZulu-Natal to international festivals.

As the founder of Zintsomi — South Africa's leading storytelling hub — Dr. Sasa has built an institution that serves storytellers, educators, children, and organisations. Zintsomi means "story" in isiXhosa. It is a living archive and launching pad for African narrative.

Her books — including the beloved Khalulu: The Story Doll series — are used in classrooms across the country. Her research informs curriculum development at multiple universities. And now, her life's work is becoming the foundation of Africa's first WebXR storytelling metaverse.
